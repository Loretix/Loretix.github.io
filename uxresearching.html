Shared Housing Study üè†

This research project explored the dynamics of shared apartments, identifying the main causes of conflict and opportunities for improvement. The methodology was divided into several structured phases, starting with a desk research and progressing through user profiling, benchmarking, and interview preparation.

1

Desk Research

Comprehensive analysis of the current situation in shared housing, identifying common conflicts such as cleanliness, organization, and lack of privacy. Relevant data was obtained from reliable sources, including housing surveys from Fotocasa.

2

Benchmarking

Review of existing solutions and tools aimed at improving cohabitation. This helped detect gaps in the market and define opportunities for new approaches.

3

Target Users

  • Primary Users ‚Äì University students, young professionals, newly arrived foreigners, and single adults.
  • Secondary Users ‚Äì Older adults and people with low interest in cleanliness or organization.
4

User Analysis

Development of Travis Matrix and proto-personas to visualize user needs, motivations, and potential friction points in shared living scenarios.

5

Interview Preparation

Design of a semi-structured interview plan, including screener questions, informed consent, and detailed interview scripts to ensure consistent data collection.
